Home brief card. Stacked vs inline
Both variants lead with the title so the card is immediately identifiable as a daily brief. Stacked is more newspaper-masthead; inline mirrors the email subject pattern and saves a row. Pick one and we lock.
Option 1

Stacked

Title on its own line at 16px, date beneath at 12px muted. Report-style. Title gets full visual weight.

Daily brief
Friday, April 18
Markets found solid ground this morning. Bitcoin held above $94k while equities opened mixed on Fed minutes...
Option 2

Inline

Title and date on same line, separator in muted gray. Newsletter-style. Matches email subject pattern. Date uses short format to prevent wrapping.

Daily brief · Fri, Apr 18
Markets found solid ground this morning. Bitcoin held above $94k while equities opened mixed on Fed minutes...
Full brief page

Always stacked (room to breathe). Full date format since it's the standalone view.

Daily brief

Friday, April 18
My read
Inline (Option 2) wins for the home card. It matches the email pattern you already ship (consistent mental model across surfaces), saves a row of vertical space on a busy home feed, and still leads with the title. The short date ("Fri, Apr 18") is enough context; full date lives on the full brief page where users land when they want detail.
Full brief page stays stacked. Standalone view has space. Large title + date beneath reads as the canonical "this is today's brief" header.
"Daily brief" vs "The Daily brief"
Recommend "Daily brief". Scales better across 5+ surfaces (home card, full page, email subject, Telegram push, notification). "The Daily brief" would only make sense if we wanted an editorial masthead identity, which adds character but costs characters everywhere it appears. If you want the editorial feel later, we can upgrade to "The Daily brief" as a full-page masthead while keeping "Daily brief" everywhere else. Not worth doing now.
