Design notes
Toggle: full-width segmented control with 2px black border. Matches plan cards visually so it reads as a peer control, not an afterthought. "Save 17%" amber pill is attached to the Annual tab so the incentive is visible without selecting it.
Default: Monthly. Aligns with "default to monthly" direction and eliminates the price-jump surprise Sarah hit.
Trial banner: sits above the toggle so the trial framing is absorbed before the user compares prices. Second reinforcement via "7-day trial" chip inside each plan card.
Feature list: replaces the dash-separated one-liner (3 assets · Manual · 3x · No fee) with a checked list. Each row has a bold key + muted detail, which Sarah's "tell me what I'm buying" read needs.
Asset count: first feature row, bold, with a concrete examples ("crypto, equities, commodities"). Directly addresses Sarah not realising equities existed and not knowing Standard was capped.
Skip link: small, single-line ghost link. With Free killed, this routes to a brief-only preview mode (not a "Free plan"). Needs routing decision — see open questions.
CTA copy: "Start 7-day trial" (not "Subscribe to Standard"). Confirms trial framing and lowers commitment feel at the click moment.
Open questions for this wireframe
1. Trial entitlement: does the trial mirror that specific plan (Standard trial = 8 assets, manual approval) or does every trial give Premium entitlements with downgrade on day 8?

2. Payment collection: take card details up-front for the trial, or no card required? Up-front = better conversion to paid, worse signup conversion. No card = opposite.

3. "Skip for now" destination: brief-only preview of the app, or marketing site? If preview, needs a clear upgrade banner at the top at all times.

4. Trial banner wording: "Nothing charged until day 8" assumes card-up-front. If no card, change to "Add card anytime during trial."
