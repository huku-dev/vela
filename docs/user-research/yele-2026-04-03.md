## Findings

### Account / Trade flow

#### [BLOCKER · Bug] "Enable Trading" doesn't surface deposit/withdraw or wallet address

> "Upon attempting to 'Enable Trading,' a bug was discovered where the expected deposit and withdrawal buttons and the wallet address were not displayed."

**What's happening:** When a free-tier user clicks Enable Trading, the deposit/withdraw affordances and wallet address fail to render. The user is stuck — they can see the call to action but cannot take the next step.

**Why it matters:** A free user who came to fund and trade hits a dead end at the moment they decided to engage. This is the worst possible failure mode: a user who already raised their hand can't follow through.

**Suggested action:** Investigate the conditional render logic on Enable Trading for free tier. Likely related to wallet provisioning state (see next finding) — the UI may be gating display on a wallet record that hasn't been written.

---

#### [BLOCKER · Bug] Privy creates the embedded wallet on Google Connect but doesn't register it to the user profile

> "the wallet was created by Privy upon Google connect but was not yet registered to the user profile."

**What's happening:** Privy's embedded-wallet creation runs at OAuth completion, but the link between the new wallet and the Vela user profile (presumably the `user_wallets` row) isn't being persisted. Downstream systems that read the wallet from the profile (deposit monitor, Enable Trading UI) fail because the row is missing.

**Why it matters:** This is the upstream cause of the Enable Trading dead end above. It also breaks deposit monitoring entirely for these users — even if they figured out a workaround to deposit, the system wouldn't see the funds.

**Suggested action:** Trace the Privy `onComplete` / wallet-provisioned callback through to the `user_wallets` insert. Likely a missed `await` or a webhook race where the user-profile row is created before the wallet write fires. Add a defensive backfill on next user-load: if profile exists and no wallet, attempt provisioning idempotently.

### Onboarding

#### [HIGH · UI] Passkey option appears on signup for new users

> "the onboarding process was confusing due to the presence of a 'pass key' option which should only be for existing users."

**What's happening:** The auth screen surfaces a "Passkey" option to first-time users who have no passkey to use. Yele paused trying to figure out whether they should pick it.

**Why it matters:** First-touch friction on a flow where every second of hesitation costs conversion. The option is irrelevant for the new-user path and adds cognitive load.

**Suggested action:** Hide the Passkey option for unauthenticated sessions that haven't previously seen one. Either Privy controls this directly, or wrap the Privy auth UI to filter the methods shown. Users with an existing passkey can still get to it via "Sign in" → existing-account flow.

### Trade flow / Home

#### [HIGH · UI/Behavior] No clear affordance to execute a trade, even with "Your Trades" tab visible

> "Yele Bademosi then reported a lack of clarity on how to execute a trade, even though they saw the 'Your Trades' tab."

**What's happening:** Yele saw signals on Home, navigated to Your Trades, but couldn't tell how to actually place or accept a trade. The semi-auto flow (Vela proposes → user accepts) wasn't discoverable from the surfaces they were on.

**Why it matters:** This is a "where do I click to get the value" problem. The signals are visible, the trades tab is visible, but the bridge between them — "this is how a proposal becomes an executed trade" — isn't surfaced. Users can read the product but not use it.

**Suggested action:** Two angles worth considering: (a) when a green/red signal fires, surface a "Vela proposed a trade — review" card on Home that links into the proposal flow; (b) on Asset Detail, add an explicit primary CTA when the signal is actionable rather than implying it through the chip. Tighter integration between signal display and trade entry.

### Asset detail

#### [MED · UI] Technical indicators in "Why we think this" lack price context

> "Yele Bademosi found the technical language used in the 'Why we think this' section confusing without context, indicating that indicators needed to be positioned more clearly."

**What's happening:** The "Why we think this" expandable explainer references indicators (RSI, ADX, EMAs) before the user has seen the price levels those indicators relate to. Without a current-price anchor, the indicators read as floating numbers.

**Why it matters:** The Batch 6 jargon sweep (commit b813207) softened the words, but this is a layout/order problem the words alone can't solve. Reader-grade UX requires the anchor to land before the analysis.

**Suggested action:** Reorder the AssetDetail page so the "Where price stands" / key-price-levels block lands BEFORE the indicator-driven "Why we think this." The indicators then read as evidence supporting the price story rather than abstract numbers.

---

#### [MED · UI/New feature] Price context missing when reading moving-average analysis

> "price context is important when reading technical analysis, such as seeing the current price in relation to moving averages."

**What's happening:** When Vela mentions an EMA, SMA, or moving average level, the page doesn't show the current price next to it visually. Users have to mentally cross-reference.

**Why it matters:** Related to the previous finding but more specific. Even after the indicators are repositioned, individual indicator readings need a co-located reference number.

**Suggested action:** Inline the current price next to each indicator readout, formatted as "Above EMA-21 at $X (price now: $Y, +Z% above)." The "TriggerCard" component already does this for bull/bear levels; extend the pattern to the indicator block. Yele explicitly didn't ask for full charts (Henry's no-chart philosophy is fine), just numeric context.

### Plan / Pricing

#### [MED · Copy] "One trade" is ambiguous — Yele read it as buy + sell

> "Yele Bademosi found the term 'one trade' confusing, interpreting it as one buy and one sell, which Henry Uku confirmed is the correct interpretation."

**What's happening:** The free-plan teaser "one test trade" reads ambiguously. Yele's interpretation (buy + sell as a round trip) was actually correct, but they had to ask to confirm. Multiple users probably get this wrong silently.

**Why it matters:** Pricing copy that confuses is a conversion tax. Even when the user lands on the right meaning, the question itself dampens trust.

**Suggested action:** Reword the free-plan callout to be self-explanatory. Options: "One free trade (buy + sell on BTC)" or "Try one full BTC trade — buy and sell, no fees." The parenthetical removes ambiguity in 4 words.

---

#### [LOW · UI] Collapse/expand on plan description feels unnecessary on Free

> "the collapse/expand feature on the plan description was not necessary, as the free plan was limited to only one asset, making them want to upgrade."

**What's happening:** The plan card has a collapse/expand affordance for its feature list. Yele found it superfluous on the Free card because the limit was already obvious.

**Why it matters:** Mild UX overhead. Worth noting that Yele framed this positively — the limit visibility itself was effective in driving upgrade intent. So the underlying mechanic (clear limits) is working; the collapse adds nothing.

**Suggested action:** Drop the collapse on Free (always show the full feature list — it's only 3 lines). Standard and Premium can keep collapse if their feature lists exceed a height threshold. Low priority; ship in a polish batch.

### Track record

#### [HIGH · Bug] Inaccurate data on Track Record page

> "[Henry Uku] Fix Track Record: Correct the inaccurate data displayed within the track record section."

**What's happening:** Henry committed to fixing inaccurate data in the Track Record section. The Gemini notes don't capture exactly what Yele saw — but Henry's commitment to fix indicates Yele flagged data that didn't match expectations or was internally inconsistent.

**Why it matters:** Track record is a trust artefact. Inaccurate numbers on a "see how Vela has performed" page are uniquely damaging — users explicitly came to verify performance and found the verification surface was wrong.

**Suggested action:** Audit the Track Record query against source of truth (proposals, executions, positions tables). Likely candidates for inaccuracy: cumulative P&L summing closed + open positions inconsistently, win-rate filtering, or stale signal-vs-execution joins. Needs a fresh look at `TrackRecord.tsx` data hooks and the upstream view.
