# User research · Lota · Onboarding

**Date:** 2026-05-05
**Source:** transcript paste (Whisper-CLI transcription of Zoom audio, ~30min, ~4,600 words) + Gemini Notes summary cross-reference
**Source format:** Mixed — verbatim Whisper transcript (with occasional homophones) plus a Gemini Notes summary used to disambiguate marketing-site vs in-app moments. Quotes are participant's actual words from the verbatim transcript, lightly cleaned for readability without changing meaning.
**Participant context:** Lota — technical peer, fellow founder/builder. Uses Cursor + Claude in his own product, runs mixed-LLM stacks (Nvidia free APIs, Kimi, Llama, DeepSeek) for cost reasons. Familiar with crypto and equities, references Binance's social feed as a known pattern. Created a test account via Google auth during the call. ## Snapshot

- **10 findings** — 1 BLOCKER, 5 HIGH, 3 MED, 1 LOW
- **Surfaces touched:** Marketing site, Onboarding, Plan / Pricing, Home, Daily brief, Account (Track Record), Free-tier wallet UI, Cross-cutting

## Findings

### Marketing site

#### [HIGH · UI] Marketing home does not answer "what can Vela do for me?" or "how do I know it works?"

> "There's nothing here that's so unique about Vela. I want to see just, what can Vela do for me? It's not clear on the home page... and what Vela has done?"

**What's happening:** The marketing site leads with brand / product description rather than concrete proof. Lota's question after spending time on the site was still "what does this do for me?" Proof of trades and performance is not surfaced where it's needed: at the top of the funnel, before signup.

**Why it matters:** This is the entry point. If a technical peer who's already in the target persona can't answer "does this make me money" after reading, retail visitors won't either. This is positioning, not just layout.

**Suggested action:** Restructure the marketing home around two answers: "what Vela has done" (recent winning trades, track record) and "what Vela can do for you" (the system-triggered model in plain English). Keep narrative content but below the fold. Wireframe in HTML before building per project rule.

---

#### [MED · UI] Trade-approval screenshot should appear earlier in the marketing flow

> "That trigger, what triggers that... whenever there's a signal." (Lota walking through the screenshots in order, hitting the approval screenshot last.)

**What's happening:** The marketing site shows screenshots in an order where the trade-approval screen (which is the moment that explains the system-triggered model) appears third or later. By that point Lota had already formed wrong expectations about what the product does.

**Why it matters:** The approval screenshot is the single clearest visual explanation of the product model. Showing it later means visitors form the wrong mental model first and have to unlearn it.

**Suggested action:** Move the trade-approval / signal-arriving screenshot earlier in the marketing scroll. Pair with one-line copy that names the model: "We send a signal. You approve, or let it run on auto."

### Onboarding

#### [MED · Bug] No back button on the trial / pricing screen, and going back lands on an error

> "There's no back button from this try premium free this trial... you click back and then it took you to an error screen."

**What's happening:** The reworked plan-select screen is missing a back affordance. Browser-back from this screen, for a user who already has an account, lands on an error. Host already flagged it as a regression from the recent rework.

**Why it matters:** Users mid-decision on the trial CTA need a graceful exit. An error screen on back looks broken and costs intent at the highest-leverage moment in the funnel.

**Suggested action:** Add a back button to the pricing / trial screen. Make browser-back return to a sane previous state for both anon and authed sessions. Add a regression test for the authed re-entry path.

---

#### [HIGH · Copy] Onboarding does not explain the system-triggered model up front; users land expecting to trade manually

> "The single biggest gap that I've seen in almost every onboarding session is that people come and they're expecting, okay, where do I start trading? Because that's the norm with an investing focused product."

> "Yes, I expected to see ready signals... but I guess that's not how the product works."

**What's happening:** Vela's mental model — system generates signals, system executes trades when wallet is funded, user approves in semi-auto or sits back in full-auto — is not communicated up front in onboarding. Every onboarding-call participant has hit this. Until Lota understood the model mid-call, nothing on the page made sense to him.

**Why it matters:** When the user reads the product through the wrong frame, the product looks broken. This is the root cause of several other findings (free flow confusion, "where do I trade", "Enable Trading button doesn't work"). Fixing this fixes their downstream symptoms.

**Suggested action:** Front-load the model in onboarding. One screen, plain English: "Vela watches markets and sends signals. In semi-auto, you approve each trade. In full-auto, Vela trades when your wallet is funded. You don't trade manually here." Pair with the demo (below) so the model is shown by example, not just told.

### Plan / Pricing

#### [HIGH · New feature] Replace free tier with a demo mode (decided on the call)

> "I don't think free is any good for you. It's just so complex to figure it out. Yeah, it's useless. That's why I don't want it. But I have to, just to avoid certain types of churn."

> "They always have demo things... You can actually do like a walkthrough. Nothing is real. Demo mode with fake numbers, real flow, just to say, ah, this is what it works like. Excitement in there. But if you come here, it's like, oh, this doesn't work. It's more frustrating than not."

**What's happening:** Free tier today drops users onto a dashboard where signals are locked, trading is gated, wallet funding is unavailable end-to-end, and the product cannot demonstrate its core promise. Host acknowledged he never fully designed the free flow. Lota suggested replacing it entirely with a demo mode (fake numbers, scripted signal, mock approval, mock outcome). Host had the flow in his head by end of call and committed to building it this week.

**Why it matters:** Free is currently a worse first impression than no free at all. Demo mode preserves the "try before you buy" function without the broken-product feel.

**Suggested action:** Build the demo: realistic-looking signal arrives, walkthrough of approval, mock trade execution, mock outcome. Make the demo nature visible (small "Demo" tag) so it's not deceptive. Replace the free dashboard with this. Order the marketing CTA so non-payers land in demo, not in a locked dashboard.

### Home (in-app)

#### [HIGH · UI] In-app home should lead with "what Vela has done" — past trades / performance proof

> "I want to see signal on the homepage that has some sort of trade proof. That tells me kind of like how the system has performed on past trades."

**What's happening:** In-app home leads with the daily brief / news. Lota — and host noted his sister gave the same feedback last week — wanted the home to lead with proof (track record, recent successful trades) and what the system can do for the user, with news as a deeper layer. Host already had a redesign queued and explicitly said this call validated the direction.

**Why it matters:** First impression of an investing-adjacent product needs to answer "does this make me money?" before "what's happening in markets today?" News-first is a positioning choice that's working against the rest of the product.

**Suggested action:** Redesign in-app home with a top section that shows recent trade outcomes and performance proof. Keep the daily brief as a second section. Coordinate with the Track Record fix (data correctness) before this lands.

### Daily brief

#### [HIGH · UI] Brief is too text-heavy; participant wanted numbers first, news as expandable subtext

> "I think this brief should have been more less text, more about numbers... I care about the news, but I think by now I get the essence of it. So I care about the news... but it shouldn't be the first thing. Cause it's like, I'm already fatigued. I don't want to read it."

**What's happening:** Lota got reading-fatigued and skipped the body of the brief. He wanted a numeric / chart summary up top with narrative news as supporting subtext he could opt into.

**Why it matters:** Brief is meant to be Vela's daily hook. If returning users skim past it because the format is text-dense, the surface fails its job. Same shape as the home-page finding (numbers first, narrative second).

**Suggested action:** Restructure: lead with a numeric / chart summary block, push narrative below, treat news as expandable rather than primary.

### Account / Track Record

#### [BLOCKER · Bug] Track Record shows inconsistent and wrong numbers on a user-visible page

> "Win rate is closer to like 40, 48. So 48% average return is 2.7%. So it's like... it's all over the place. The data is rubbish."

**What's happening:** Track Record surfaces conflicting numbers — a win rate label sitting next to an average return where the units / scale don't reconcile, and host said the underlying query is pulling wrong fields. Host knew about it; users see it today.

**Why it matters:** Track Record is the single highest-trust surface. Wrong numbers there are a credibility break, not a polish issue. A user who notices loses faith in everything else the product claims. If the home redesign repositions Track Record to home, this becomes a release blocker for that work.

**Suggested action:** Audit the Track Record query. Confirm what each metric is actually computing vs what the labels promise. Either fix the numbers or hide the page until the data is correct. Block the home redesign until this lands.

### Free-tier UI

#### [MED · Bug] Free-tier shows a connected wallet address and an "Enable Trading" button that don't function

> (Per Gemini Notes summary, cross-checked against transcript context discussing wallet funding gated behind paid: "even this enabled trading here now that you have it won't show you... Even that's not your route... that shouldn't be able to be showing yourself.")

**What's happening:** On the free tier, the dashboard renders a connected wallet address and an "Enable Trading" affordance, but neither leads to a working flow. The wallet address shown is incorrect for free users; the Enable Trading button doesn't do what its label promises because trading is gated.

**Why it matters:** Showing a wallet address creates the impression of a funded, ready-to-trade account that doesn't exist. The button is a dead end. Both are trust hits and likely support tickets.

**Suggested action:** On free, hide the wallet address and the Enable Trading affordance, or clearly mark them as "available on paid." May be obsoleted if the demo-mode replacement lands first — coordinate scope with that batch.

---

#### [MED · UI] Free-tier signal cards show as locked; intended state was direction-visible

> "[The product] is supposed to show you the signal for every single asset. It's not like even the ones that are not paid... So it should show the same signal for ETH, and all this is locked everything. So I wanted that to be free."

**What's happening:** Free-tier signal cards render as locked when host's design intent was that the signal direction (BUY / SELL / WAIT) be visible to free users with the trade action gated. Implementation has diverged from intent.

**Why it matters:** Free users see only locks, can't form an opinion about whether the signals are good, and have no reason to upgrade. Compounds with the broader free-flow finding.

**Suggested action:** Verify the free-tier signal-card state. Show direction and indicator readout to free users; gate trade execution and detailed brief content. Likely obsoleted if the demo-mode replacement lands — coordinate with that work.

### Cross-cutting

#### [LOW · UI] Equities should be more prominent for non-crypto-native users

> "These are assets I'm familiar with. So nothing like, oh god, okay, this interesting one. Maybe if equities were up, like for me specifically, because I already know what the signals with crypto is."

**What's happening:** Lota — already crypto-fluent — said equity signals were where Vela could surprise him with new insight. Crypto signals he could intuit. He suggested equities should get more prominence, especially for users who aren't crypto-native.

**Why it matters:** Vela's positioning is multi-asset, not crypto-only. If the home and asset list lean visually crypto-first, that's a lost wedge for the equities-leaning ICP.

**Suggested action:** Audit asset prominence on home and the asset class filter. Consider an equities-default or equities-first variant for users whose onboarding signaled equity interest. Low priority; revisit during the home redesign.

---

### Out of scope / parked

#### [LOW · New feature] Binance-style social feed of curators and follow-able trade leaders

> "What this product reminds me of is on Binance, if you go to the comments, they have feed... they will tell you to follow, to subscribe to certain trade ads because you see what they've done."

**What's happening:** Lota compared Vela favorably to Binance's social feed where users follow curators and see trade rationales from the community.

**Why it matters:** Validates that proof-of-trades and credibility surfaces resonate. Not a feature ask Vela should chase as a copy of Binance — Vela's positioning is the system, not the crowd.

**Suggested action:** Park. Use as input for the home redesign (proof matters, follow-able outcomes matter) but don't build a community feed.

## Praise / things working well

- Post-paid onboarding flow felt clear: "After you pay, it then says, this is what... it kind of is more clear. It's like, you need to fund your wallet so that when Vela has a trading signal, it will tell you."
- Signal display itself (BUY/SELL/WAIT semantics on the asset card) was readable to a technical user without explanation.
- Seven-day-trial / paid-default flow endorsed: Lota agreed with the "high intent" framing and accepted the friction as a positive filter.
- Privy-only auth (no external wallet connect required) was understood without explanation.
- Default-to-monthly fix from the prior iteration (away from annual default) read as the right call — confirmed by Lota.
