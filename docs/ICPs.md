## Product Vision Context

Vela is a **market intelligence platform**, not a crypto trading tool. Crypto is the starting asset class, but the product expands to commodities, equities, forex, and other markets. All ICPs below should be understood through this lens — Vela helps people understand and navigate *markets*, not just crypto.

**Two equally important value propositions:**
1. **Actionable signals** — Watch markets 24/7, surface trading opportunities, execute with user approval
2. **Market literacy** — Deconstruct markets, make them less intimidating, help users genuinely understand what drives price movements and why

The litmus test for Vela's informational value: **would users still pay for Standard/Premium just for the briefs, digests, and asset analysis — even if they never placed a single trade?** If yes, the product is working. The daily briefs, asset detail pages, events-moving-markets, and all informational surfaces are not supporting features for trading — they are core product in their own right.

## ICP 1: The Time-Starved Professional

**Who they are:**
- 30-45 years old, full-time professional (tech, finance, consulting, medicine)
- Has $10K-$100K across markets — crypto today, but interested in gold, equities, commodities
- Bought crypto during a bull run, holds some index funds, maybe dabbles in commodities
- Checks prices 2-3x/day on their phone but doesn't "trade" in any structured way
- Reads the Financial Times or Bloomberg headlines but doesn't have time to go deeper

**Their frustration:**
- Missed a major move because they were in a meeting / asleep / on vacation
- Knows they should pay more attention but literally doesn't have the hours
- Tried setting price alerts but gets too many or too few — no context, just noise
- Feels like they're always reacting to markets rather than understanding them
- Reads a headline like "Gold surges on inflation fears" and thinks "what does that actually mean for me?"

**What they want from Vela:**
- Someone watching while they're not — and explaining what matters in plain language
- Not a firehose of alerts. One clear signal: "This is happening, here's what it means, here's what you could do"
- To actually understand why markets move — not just that they moved. The daily brief over morning coffee that makes them feel informed, not overwhelmed
- Confidence that they won't miss the next big move, across any asset class
- Control — they approve every trade, nothing happens without them

**How they'd describe Vela to a friend:**
"It watches the markets for me, explains what's happening in plain English, and tells me when something actually matters. I approve everything — it never trades without me. Honestly, even the daily briefs alone are worth the subscription."

**Tier fit:** Standard ($10/mo). Values the time savings and market understanding more than the cost. Upgrade to Premium when they see the track record and want faster signals + more assets.

**Where to reach them:**
- X (finance/markets adjacent accounts), LinkedIn
- Facebook ads targeting trading app users + finance interest + 30-45 age
- Referral from a friend who trades or reads the briefs

## ICP 2: The Market Curious

**Who they are:**
- 22-32 years old, often in tech or creative industries
- Has $500-$10K invested, mostly crypto on Coinbase/Robinhood, maybe a Vanguard account
- Interested in markets broadly but overwhelmed by the complexity — "I know I should learn more about investing"
- Follows some finance accounts but finds most content either too basic ("buy Bitcoin!") or too technical ("EMA-9 crossed the SMA-50")
- Hears colleagues talk about gold, oil, or S&P 500 moves and feels out of the loop

**Their frustration:**
- Doesn't know technical analysis and doesn't want to learn it from textbooks
- Feels like they need to "get smarter" before they can invest actively
- Markets feel like a members-only club with its own language
- Worried about getting scammed or making a rookie mistake
- Every time they've tried to time the market, they've lost money

**What they want from Vela:**
- The barrier to entry removed — plain English, not jargon, across all asset classes
- To feel like they're getting smarter every day just by using the product. The briefs, the "events moving markets" breakdowns, the asset context — all of it making markets less scary
- A feeling of "oh, someone smart is helping me" without the intimidation
- Education embedded naturally into the product, not as homework
- Low stakes to start (free tier, view-only, paper trading)

**How they'd describe Vela to a friend:**
"It explains what's happening in markets like a smart friend would. I actually understand why Bitcoin dropped or why gold is up now. And when I'm ready, I can trade through it too."

**Tier fit:** Free initially. The informational value — briefs making them feel informed, asset pages demystifying what drives prices — is what builds the habit. Converts to Standard after 2-4 weeks when they realize "I open this every morning." The "Vela caught this move" moment accelerates conversion, but the daily learning is what retains them.

**Where to reach them:**
- TikTok (education content, "here's what just happened in 30 seconds")
- X (engagement posts, polls, accessible explainers about markets broadly)
- Reddit (r/investing, r/stocks, r/cryptocurrency — genuine participation, not promo)
- Word of mouth from ICP 1

## ICP 3: The Independent Retail Trader

**Who they are:**
- 28-40 years old, actively trades across markets (crypto, stocks, forex, commodities)
- $25K-$250K portfolio, trades several times per week
- Uses TradingView, Coinalyze, or Bloomberg Terminal access
- Knows indicators (RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands) but may not have a systematic approach
- Has been burned by paid signal groups and "alpha" Telegram channels
- Already watches multiple asset classes and wants coverage across all of them

**Their frustration:**
- Spends too much time staring at charts and second-guessing entries/exits
- Knows about indicators but can't combine them into a consistent system
- Doesn't trust most signal services because they hide their track record
- Wants a second opinion, not a replacement for their own judgment
- Market context is scattered across 5 different apps and newsletters

**What they want from Vela:**
- A systematic, transparent signal engine they can evaluate objectively — across asset classes, not just crypto
- Full track record visibility — wins AND losses, no cherry-picking
- Multi-asset coverage so they're not missing opportunities in markets they don't personally watch
- The "why" behind every signal — Vela's briefs and analysis give them context that complements their own charting
- Speed — they want to act on signals quickly, especially with leverage

**How they'd describe Vela to a friend:**
"It's a market intelligence engine that actually shows its track record. The signals are solid, but honestly the daily analysis across assets is what keeps me coming back — it catches context I miss when I'm focused on one chart."

**Tier fit:** Goes straight to Standard or Premium. Evaluates based on track record transparency first, then signal quality. Price is irrelevant if signals are good — $20/mo is nothing relative to their portfolio. The informational depth (briefs, market context, cross-asset analysis) is what differentiates Vela from bare signal services and keeps them subscribed.

**Where to reach them:**
- X (signal showcase posts with real P&L, build-in-public transparency)
- Trading forums and Discord servers (genuine participation)
- "Vela's track record" content — this ICP evaluates before buying

| Notification speed | Values it but won't realize it matters | Doesn't care yet | Cares a lot — minutes matter |

## The Informational Value Test

For each ICP, the question is: **would they pay for Vela without ever trading?**

- **ICP 1:** Yes — the daily brief replacing 30 minutes of scattered news consumption is worth $10/mo alone. "I feel informed without doing the work."
- **ICP 2:** Yes — this is their primary use case. Vela makes markets less intimidating. Trading is a future upgrade, not the entry point. "I'm actually learning."
- **ICP 3:** Probably not alone — they need signals too. But the informational layer is what differentiates Vela from commodity signal bots and prevents churn. "Other services just say buy/sell. Vela tells me why."

**Product implication:** The briefs, digests, asset detail pages, and educational content are not secondary to trading — they are a standalone value proposition that must be excellent on their own terms. Every informational surface should pass the test: "Would someone pay for just this?"

## Anti-Personas (Who Vela is NOT For)

**The Degen Gambler**
- Wants 100x leveraged meme coin plays
- "Wen moon?" mindset, no interest in risk management or understanding markets
- Would ignore WAIT signals and complain about missed pumps

**The Quant / Algo Trader**
- Already has their own backtested systems
- Wants raw data feeds and API access, not plain English
- Would find Vela's approach too simple

**The Market Skeptic**
- Doesn't believe in active investing or market intelligence
- "Just buy index funds" — which is fine, but Vela isn't for them
- Not our job to convert them

## Using This Document

- **Writing social posts:** Match voice to the ICP you're targeting. Education posts → ICP 2. Signal showcase → ICP 3. "Never miss a move" → ICP 1. Market explainers → all three.
- **Feature prioritization:** Weight decisions toward ICP 1 (largest addressable market, highest willingness to pay for time savings). But never deprioritize informational quality — it's what ICP 2 pays for and what retains ICP 3.
- **Marketing spend:** Facebook ads → ICP 1. TikTok → ICP 2. X organic → all three, especially ICP 3.
- **Onboarding flow:** ICP 2 needs more hand-holding and should see briefs/digest value immediately. ICP 3 wants to skip to the track record. ICP 1 wants "show me value in 30 seconds."
- **Multi-asset expansion messaging:** Lead with "Vela now watches gold/equities/etc" — not "Vela now trades gold." The intelligence comes first, trading follows.
- **Content quality bar:** Every brief, every digest, every asset page should be good enough that someone would screenshot it and share it. That's the organic growth engine for ICP 2 especially.
