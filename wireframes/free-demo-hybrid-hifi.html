Hybrid demo flow — high-fidelity variants

Locked the direction at hybrid. Now exploring how each surface should actually look at production fidelity. Each section below shows variants for one screen / decision so we can compare the feel side-by-side, not just the structure.

Headings in Space Grotesk 800. Body in Inter. 3-4px black borders, solid neobrutalist shadows. Three-pillar voice (Always Watching · You Stay in Control · Plain English), first-person ("I" for Vela). Logomark + lowercase wordmark per ADR-006. Tagline: "Smarter trading starts here."

Open questions for you

  1. Section 1 (carousel): A, B, or C? Reconsidered after the brand pass — C now my pick because it explicitly maps all three pillars (Always Watching, You Stay in Control, Plain English) on one screen, in the brand's first-person voice. A is still strong if we want product-feel up front. B is brand-led but most removed from the model.
  2. Section 2 (banner): A, B, or C? Amber feels like a warning; ink-dark reads as branding; pill-only is the quietest. My pick is B (ink) — pairs naturally with the sticky CTA.
  3. Section 3 (approve flow): Inline coachmark or pause modal? My pick is A (inline). Modal is patronizing for a tech-fluent ICP.
  4. Section 4 (outcome pacing): Live-and-resolve over 30s, or fast-forward to closed? My pick is A — the 30s wait is the moment Lota was missing ("the system has to prove to you that it works"). But B is faster + simpler to build.
  5. Always-win vs randomized outcome: still open from the previous wireframe. Curated set of past wins (and a couple of cleanly-stopped losses) feels most honest. Suggestion: ship always-win first, add randomized in v2 if it's a real concern.
  6. Reset on visit vs persist for session: still open. Suggestion: persist for the session via localStorage, fresh on a new device. Returning users land back in "watching it play out" instead of starting over.
